The Digital Divide

By Christopher Gaona

The digital divide has created a gap in the world for a long time now and it still continues years after the World Wide Web has been out. Technology ownership has been growing rapidly, especially in the United States, but there is still a large population that is still affected by this.

  • Lower-Income Americans Around 3 in 10 adults with a household income below $30,000 a year don't own a smartphone. And more than 4 in 10 adults don't have home internet service or a personal computer.
  • Relying on Smartphones A lot of lower-income Americans are relying on smartphones to do task that someone usually does on a computer, laptop, or tablet.
  • Homework Gap This gap also affects kids who have homework that is online as many school-age children from lower-income households do not have internet at home.

Sources Pew Research Center
